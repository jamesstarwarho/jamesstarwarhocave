Sustainable Development

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a set of 17 global goals established by the United Nations in September 2015 as part of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. The SDGs are designed to address various social, economic, and environmental challenges facing the world and are intended to guide global efforts toward a more sustainable and equitable future. The goals cover a wide range of issues, including poverty, hunger, health, education, gender equality, clean water, sanitation, affordable and clean energy, decent work, industry innovation, reduced inequalities, sustainable cities, responsible consumption and production, climate action, life below water, life on land, peace, justice, and partnerships for the goals.

Each goal is accompanied by specific targets and indicators to measure progress. The aim is to achieve these goals by 2030, promoting a more inclusive, sustainable, and prosperous world for present and future generations. The SDGs emphasize the interconnectedness of social, economic, and environmental issues, recognizing that addressing one goal often involves addressing multiple aspects of development.

History

The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development ,unanimously adopted by all United Nations Member States in 2015, outlines a collective framework for fostering global peace and prosperity for current and future generations. Central to this agenda are the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), representing a compelling call to action for all nations, both developed and developing, to collaborate in a worldwide partnership. The SDGs recognize the interconnectedness of eradicating poverty and addressing other forms of deprivation alongside efforts to enhance health and education, reduce inequality, promote economic growth, and concurrently address climate change while preserving our oceans and forests.

The foundation of the SDGs builds upon extensive work carried out by countries and the UN, including initiatives by the UN Department of Economic and Social Affairs:

  • In June 1992, during the Earth Summit in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, over 178 countries adopted Agenda 21, a comprehensive plan of action aiming to establish a global partnership for sustainable development, enhancing human lives, and safeguarding the environment.
  • At the Millennium Summit in September 2000 in New York, Member States unanimously endorsed the Millennium Declaration, leading to the formulation of eight Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) designed to alleviate extreme poverty by 2015.
  • The World Summit on Sustainable Development in South Africa in 2002 resulted in the adoption of the Johannesburg Declaration on Sustainable Development and the Plan of Implementation. This reaffirmed global commitments to poverty eradication and environmental protection, building on Agenda 21 and the Millennium Declaration while placing increased emphasis on multilateral partnerships.
  • The United Nations Conference on Sustainable Development (Rio+20) in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, in June 2012, witnessed the adoption of "The Future We Want", an outcome document that initiated the process of developing SDGs to build upon the MDGs. It also established the UN High-level Political Forum on Sustainable Development.
  • In 2013, the General Assembly established a 30-member Open Working Group to propose the SDGs.
  • The negotiation process on the post-2015 development agenda commenced in January 2015, culminating in the adoption of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, featuring 17 SDGs at its core, during the UN Sustainable Development Summit in September 2015.
  • The year 2015 marked a significant period for multilateralism and international policy shaping, witnessing the adoption of major agreements:
    • Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction (March 2015)
    • Addis Ababa Action Agenda on Financing for Development (July 2015)
    • Paris Agreement on Climate Change (December 2015)
  • Currently, the annual High-level Political Forum on Sustainable Development serves as the primary UN platform for the follow-up and review of the SDGs.

Today, the Division for Sustainable Development Goals (DSDG) within the United Nations Department of Economic and Social Affairs (UNDESA) provides substantive support and capacity-building for the SDGs and their related thematic issues. These include water, energy, climate, oceans, urbanization, transport, science and technology, the Global Sustainable Development Report (GSDR), partnerships and Small Island Developing States. DSDG plays a crucial role in evaluating the UN's systemwide implementation of the 2030 Agenda, as well as in advocacy and outreach activities related to the SDGs. To transform the 2030 Agenda into reality, the broad ownership of the SDGs must translate into a strong commitment by all stakeholders to implement these global goals, and DSDG aims to facilitate this engagement.
